Understanding Isaiah

A deeper look at and study of Isaiah 53

First Look

First Contact

At a first glance this text brings feelings of love and gratitude for the Savior as well as sorrow for the pains that He had to face. It describes the suffering and pain the Christ endured in order to redeem all mankind from their sins. We also see that Isaiah talks about these events from a first hand perspective while at the time of his writing they had not yet occured. Isaiah 53

The World Behind the Text

Before we dive into the text intself it is important to consider the context of the text.

The Book of Isaiah

Throughout this book of scripture found in the old testament, Isaiah the prophet speaks not only to God's people during his own day, but also to all the followers of the Lord in the days to come. He explores themes of redemption, hope, mercy, justice, righteousness. He encourages the people to follow the Lord and warns of the destruction that will come if they do not. This chapter fits in at a place where Isaiah touches on the subject of the "suffering servant‚Äù and addresses the common misconception that bad things only happen to bad people.

The Book of Mormon

Another prophet of the Lord, Abinidai, quoted the very same chapter in his own teachings. Abinidai was sent to testify of the Savior and to warn the people of the wickedness but was not well received. While standing before the wicked King Noah, with his own life at risk, Abinidai teaches the ten commandments and testifies of the Savior. He teaches from Isaiah about the suffering of the Savior and the importance of the sacrifice that Christ would make.

"But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed."

During a spiritual experience, I came to know that these words are true and understand more fully that the Savior desires to heal and help us. Isaiah 53:5

The World of the Text

Isaiah uses imagery and metaphors to describe the suffering of the Savior and His great sacrifice. The way he describes these events as if they have already happened leads us to beleive that he may have seen these things in a vision.

Repeating Themes

The word grief is repeated throughout the text which corresponds to the action words used to describe the treatment of the Savior. These words include despised, rejected, wounded, oppressed, afflicted, and bruised. Isaiah used various words to describe the way in which the Savior suffered for his fellow man, for their transgressions.

Placement in Time

This text refers to things in the past and present and future. Changes in the text

This text does not seem to undergo any change or development. It does generally start talking about the Savior growing up and ends with talking about the reward that he will receive but other than that the text seems to spend the time talking about one thing in particular.

"For he shall grow up before him as a tender plant, and as a root out of a dry ground"

This imagery helps the reader to understand that Christ came out a time where he was out of place. It could be said that there was a spiritual drought in the land and yet He "grew" in the midst of it.

"All we like sheep have gone astray"

Wether during Isaiah's lifetime, the Savior's lifetime, or our own lives, there are times when the people have wandered away from the Lord's teachings. When this happens we lose the protection of the good sheppard.

The World in Front of the Text

Gratitude

Isaiah helps the reader to understand the true pain and suffering that the Savior had to face. While initially this introduces a sense of sorrow, sorrow later becomes joy and gratitude as we understand that Christ was willing to suffer this much because of the love He and our Heavenly Father have for us.

Becoming

Through this chapter, Isaiah's words can inspire us with a desire to become more like our Savior. It shows us that it is ok to be different or to not be the most beautiful person. Christ shows us that we are not defined by the way we are treated by others, and that true value comes from the way we live our lives.

Healing

This chapter reminds me of the importance of using this wonderful gift that Christ has given us. It is with His suffering that we are healed. There is no reason I should hold onto hurt or anger. I know that if I turn to the Lord in my pain and suffering, He will understand, and He will bear my burdens with me.

The scriptures and prophecies of the prophets are as complex as they are beautiful.

We have only begun to scratch the surface of truley understanding this chapter and there is always more to learn. Continue to read, study, and ponder the words of Isaiah. As you do so the spirit will help you to understand and cherish these words. Isaiah 53
